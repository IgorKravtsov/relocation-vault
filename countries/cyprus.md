### 5.1. Legalization {status: partial, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-05-25, dod: partial}

> **DoD status**: Partial. This pass captured temporary-protection context, the official Cyprus Digital Nomad route, PR/citizenship anchors, and a first playbook. Missing: Cyprus-specific post-04 March 2027 TP conversion rule, exact treatment of an existing Polish residence title, and a document-by-document DN checklist from the linked forms package.

#### Now (until 03.2027)

**Temporary protection for Ukrainians.** The EU-level temporary-protection framework is extended to 04 March 2027 by Council Implementing Decision (EU) 2025/1460 [src-002]. The Cyprus Asylum Service page captured in this pass confirms the Cyprus local mechanics for earlier extensions: beneficiaries already granted temporary protection in Cyprus did not need a new application or new biometric residence permit for the automatic extension to 04 March 2026, while first-time applicants could apply electronically via the Asylum Service website or in person at the Asylum Service headquarters in Nicosia [src-036]. This supports the operating assumption that Cyprus implements TP extensions administratively, but the Cyprus page captured here has not yet been updated to 04 March 2027; use the EU source as the high-confidence horizon and keep the Cyprus local implementation detail under verification.

**Cyprus Digital Nomad residence permit.** Cyprus has reopened applications under the "Cyprus Digital Nomad Visa" scheme, with a cap of 500 permits [src-035]. Eligible applicants must be non-EU/non-EEA nationals who can work remotely through telecommunications technology, either for an employer registered abroad or as self-employed providers to clients abroad, and must prove stable monthly net income of at least **€3,500 after contributions and taxes** [src-035]. For this couple, that threshold is a major blocker at current income: $3,000/month gross is normally below €3,500 net.

The permit gives one year of residence, renewable for a further two years [src-035]. Family members can reside for the same period but cannot be employed or perform economic activity in Cyprus [src-035]. The official page defines family members as a spouse, a partner in a civil union, and underaged children [src-035]. A girlfriend who is not married and not in a civil union should be treated as **not safely covered** until verified; marriage or civil union may materially change feasibility.

**Application mechanics.** The DN application is filed within 3 months of arrival at the Migration Department's central offices in Nicosia, personally or through an authorised representative [src-035]. Documents accompanying application forms must be officially translated and duly certified / ratified [src-035]. Applications without all required documents are not accepted for consideration [src-035]. Biometric data and signature are captured for permit issuance [src-035]. Fees captured from the official page are €70 for issuance or renewal plus €70 for initial Aliens' Registry registration [src-035]. Examination time is 5–7 weeks [src-035].

**Polish karta pobytu interaction.** Safe planning baseline: a Polish residence title does not substitute for Cyprus residence authorization. If the Polish title is temporary protection, the EU one-Member-State TP principle remains relevant under the EU TP framework [src-002]. This Cyprus-specific interaction was not directly captured from a Cyprus source in this pass; add it to verification.

#### After 4 March 2027

Verification pass baseline: no Cyprus-specific TP-to-ordinary-residence bridge equivalent to Greece's Article 194 has been captured. Planning should therefore not rely on TP alone after 04 March 2027 unless Cyprus publishes a later transition rule. The practical fallback is to secure an ordinary route before the TP cliff if income rises enough for DN or if a different Cyprus residence basis becomes viable. The DN route can provide up to three years total residence (1 year + renewal for up to 2 years) [src-035], but the current income requirement is the limiting factor.

#### Residence without local employer

The official DN route explicitly allows work for a foreign employer or remote self-employment for clients abroad [src-035]. It does **not** allow family members to work or perform economic activity in Cyprus [src-035]. Local-employer work, visitor status, company-owner routes, and ordinary self-employment / business residence remain pending research.

#### PR and citizenship

Cyprus has an official long-term-resident forms page with application packages for acquisition and renewal of long-term-resident status [src-038], but this pass did not extract the detailed five-year eligibility rules from the linked DOCX list. Citizenship by naturalization is documented by the Ministry of Interior: adult applicants must have 12 months of legal and continuous residence immediately before application, and during the immediately preceding 10 years must have legal residence for cumulative periods of at least 7 years; they must also meet good-character, Greek B1, Cyprus civic-knowledge, accommodation, financial-resource, and intention-to-reside requirements [src-037].

#### Personal playbook for our couple

1. **Do not treat Cyprus TP as a long-term plan by itself.** EU TP runs to 04 March 2027 [src-002], but no Cyprus ordinary-residence bridge was captured.
2. **DN is only realistic if income rises.** The primary applicant needs at least €3,500/month net after taxes/contributions [src-035], above current ~$3,000/month income.
3. **If aiming for Cyprus DN, prepare proof of remote work and income before arrival.** File within 3 months of arrival at the Migration Department in Nicosia, with official translations / certification as required [src-035].
4. **Partner strategy likely requires formalization.** The official DN family definition covers spouse / civil-union partner and underage children [src-035]. Treat an unmarried girlfriend without civil union as a blocker until verified.
5. **Polish karta pobytu should be kept as a separate planning track.** It does not replace Cyprus authorization; if it is TP, avoid assuming dual TP coverage under the EU one-Member-State rule [src-002].

### 5.2. Climate {status: partial, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-05-25, dod: partial}

> **DoD status**: Partial. Temperatures, humidity, sun hours, and comfort are covered for 3+ cities from one climate source. Direct sunny-day counts remain missing and are queued for verification.

Cyprus is a strong climate fit if the couple wants warm winters, long summers, and high sunshine, but summer heat is not evenly distributed. Climates to Travel describes a Mediterranean coastal climate with mild rainy winters and hot sunny summers; inland Nicosia is hotter because the Mesaoria Plain is shielded from sea breezes by mountains [src-039].

| City | January average | July average / heat note | Humidity / sunshine | Practical comfort |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| Larnaca | ~12.5°C | ~27.8°C avg; July high ~32.8°C | Annual humidity ~65%; ~3,350 sun hours/year | Good coastal fit; long hot summer, some muggy periods, sea-breeze relief. |
| Limassol | ~13.3°C | ~27.5°C avg; July high ~32.8°C | Annual humidity ~67%; ~3,415 sun hours/year | Warm coastal city; summer is hot/muggy but moderated by breezes. |
| Nicosia | ~11.1°C | ~30.3°C avg; July high ~37.8°C | Annual humidity ~59%; ~3,300 sun hours/year | Winter mild, but inland summer heat is severe; avoid if heat-sensitive. |
| Paphos | ~13.1°C | ~25.6°C avg; July high ~30.0°C | Annual humidity ~70%; ~3,335 sun hours/year | Best first-pass comfort candidate: milder summer highs, warm winters, coastal humidity. |

Climates to Travel says the sky is almost always clear in the long summer and that the coast generally has no long rain period from June to September, when the sun always shines [src-039]. However, this is not a city-level annual sunny-day count, so the section remains partial pending a direct-statistics source.

## Block 5 — Practical verdict

- **Can relocate now**: Possibly via TP if eligible and if Cyprus implements the EU 2027 extension locally, or via DN if income is raised above €3,500/month net.
- **Best legalization path for the man**: DN only after income increase; otherwise TP is a temporary bridge, not a long-term plan.
- **Best legalization path for the woman**: TP independently if eligible; DN dependent only if spouse / civil-union partner or another confirmed family basis exists.
- **Does marriage change the picture**: Yes. Official DN family coverage includes spouse / civil-union partner, not an informal girlfriend [src-035].
- **Realism of staying after 03.2027**: Unclear / medium-low at current income until a Cyprus post-TP bridge or a feasible ordinary residence route is verified.

**Pros**:
- Very strong warm-climate fit, especially coastal cities.
- Official DN route is designed for remote foreign work.
- Citizenship by residence has a documented 7-year cumulative-residence requirement, not an indefinite route.

**Cons / risks**:
- DN income threshold (€3,500/month net) is above current income.
- No Cyprus-specific post-04 March 2027 TP bridge captured.
- Partner dependency likely requires marriage or civil union.
- Nicosia summer heat is severe.
