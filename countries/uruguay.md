## Block 3 - Profile by section

### 5.1. Legalization {status: partial, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-06-01, dod: partial}

> **DoD status**: partial. Entry, ordinary residence, DN/provisional identity, and citizenship baselines are official-source backed. Missing: exact practical approval pattern for foreign-client IT income, partner/dependent mechanics, processing times/fees, Polish `karta pobytu` interaction, and a full couple playbook.

#### Now (until 03.2027)

- **Entry**: Ukraine appears in Uruguay's 2025 admission-visa regime table as **"No necesita Visa"** for ordinary, diplomatic, and service passports. For planning, this opens visa-free tourist entry before filing an in-country status. [src-180]
- **Ordinary legal residence**: Uruguay's Ministry of Interior / National Migration Directorate says foreign nationals who want to reside temporarily or permanently can identify the applicable residence procedure and obtain Uruguayan residence plus a cedula. The general page distinguishes permanent residence, Mercosur residence, family-link residence, and non-Mercosur temporary residence. [src-181]
- **Permanent legal residence route**: the permanent-residence checklist is open to a foreigner intending to reside permanently. Core documents include valid identity document/passport, criminal-record certificates from origin and countries where the person lived 6+ months in the last 5 years, marriage/birth documents if a family link is used, photo, Uruguayan health card, vaccination certificate, and proof of means of life. Foreign documents generally need apostille/legalization and translation by a Uruguayan public translator unless electronically verifiable. [src-182]
- **Means of life for remote IT work**: the permanent-residence checklist explicitly includes an **employee of a foreign legal entity** evidence category: a notarial certificate must describe the link with the foreign company, company country/object, that it has no Uruguayan branch, income amount, payment method, how the applicant performs the activity from Uruguay, and whether registration with Uruguayan labour/social-security bodies applies. It also includes independent worker / sole-proprietor evidence through notarial and accounting certificates. [src-182]
- **Digital-nomad / provisional identity route**: Uruguay's special provisional identity sheet for digital nomads can be requested online or in person. For the first six months, the DN subcategory requires an identity document and signed sworn declaration. Government communications describe the route as a six-month stay after regular tourist entry, renewable for another six months if the applicant provides criminal-record certificates and a Uruguayan vaccination certificate. [src-183][src-185]
- **No EU temporary protection bridge issue**: Uruguay is outside the EU temporary-protection framework. The relevant post-03.2027 planning question is not TP conversion but whether the couple can establish ordinary Uruguayan residence / tax / work-status compliance before relying on Uruguay as a long-term base.

#### After 4 March 2027

- There is no Ukraine-specific TP-to-residence bridge to capture because Uruguay is not an EU TP state. The conservative baseline is to treat Uruguay as an **ordinary residence plan**: enter visa-free if still eligible, then file permanent legal residence (or start with the DN/provisional identity only as a short bridge) well before the EU TP horizon. [src-180][src-181][src-182]
- The DN/provisional identity route is not enough by itself for long-term planning: it is framed as 6 months plus a 6-month renewal, while the long-term anchor is permanent legal residence / legal citizenship. Verification baseline for `vq-059`: do not assume DN/provisional-identity time alone will satisfy habitual-residence / citizenship evidence; use it only as an entry bridge while building a permanent legal residence file. Partner mechanics should be handled through separate residence files and family/civil-status evidence, with marriage helpful for documentary clarity and possibly the family-constituted citizenship timeline. [src-182][src-183][src-184][src-185]

#### Residence without local employer

- The strongest captured official hook for the IT partner is **foreign-company employment** or **independent worker / sole proprietor** evidence under the permanent-residence means-of-life checklist. This is better than treating the digital-nomad sheet as the main path, because permanent residence is the route connected to stable residence and later citizenship evidence. [src-182]
- No fixed minimum monthly income was captured on the official page. Verification baseline for `vq-058`: for planning, this is not a fixed-threshold DN-style route; it is a documentary sufficiency file. The couple's ~$3,000/month foreign-client income is plausible only if a Uruguayan notary/accountant can certify the foreign-company or independent-worker means-of-life package, payment method, and any DGI/BPS implications. Treat notarial/accounting preparation as mandatory, not optional. [src-182]

#### PR and citizenship

- Uruguay's ordinary residence system includes a permanent legal residence procedure for non-Mercosur foreigners; unlike many countries, the first long-term target can be permanent residence rather than a multi-year temporary-permit ladder. [src-181][src-182]
- Legal citizenship is handled through the Uruguayan citizenship-letter procedure. The gub.uy page citing Constitution Article 75 says foreigners of good conduct with family constituted in Uruguay, capital/property or a science/art/industry, and 3 years of habitual residence can qualify; foreigners without family constituted need the same qualities and 5 years of habitual residence. Applicants must be 18+. [src-184]
- Habitual residence is strict: the citizenship procedure says exits over 6 consecutive months reset the required 3- or 5-year residence period after re-entry. Foreign-language applicants must understand and express themselves in Spanish. [src-184]
- Marriage may matter less for the residence filing than for the citizenship timeline: "family constituted in the Republic" can reduce the legal-citizenship residence period from 5 years to 3 years if the relationship/family evidence is accepted. Exact treatment of a newly married Ukrainian couple living together in Uruguay needs follow-up. [src-184]

#### Polish karta pobytu interaction

- No Uruguay-specific source was captured on interaction with an existing Polish residence card. Operational baseline: a Polish card does not replace Uruguayan entry/residence authorization; it may help document prior lawful residence/travel history but Uruguay will still require its own residence filing, apostilled/legalized foreign documents, and criminal records for countries of recent residence. [src-181][src-182]

#### Personal playbook for our couple

1. **Before arrival**: collect Ukrainian passports; Ukrainian and Polish criminal-record certificates if either partner lived in Poland 6+ months in the last 5 years; birth certificates; potential marriage certificate if they marry; employment/client contracts; bank statements; proof of foreign-client income; apostilles/legalizations and Spanish translations by a Uruguayan public translator where required. [src-182]
2. **Entry**: use Ukraine visa-free entry if still current, not a consular visa path. [src-180]
3. **First bridge**: if immediate residence preparation is incomplete, file the digital-nomad provisional identity route after regular entry as a short 6+6 month bridge; do not treat it as a long-term solution. [src-183][src-185]
4. **Long-term filing**: prioritize permanent legal residence using the foreign-company employee or independent-worker means-of-life category, with a Uruguayan notary/accountant package explaining income, payment method, and whether local BPS/DGI registration is required. [src-182]
5. **Partner**: marriage is likely useful for document coherence and possibly the 3-year citizenship timeline, but dependent mechanics for an unmarried partner were not captured in this pass. [src-182][src-184]
6. **Long-term**: maintain habitual residence and avoid any absence over 6 consecutive months if legal citizenship is the target. Spanish competency becomes a real requirement. [src-184]

### 5.2. Climate {status: deep, depth: 2, last_updated: 2026-06-01, dod: passed}

> **DoD status**: passed at medium confidence. Temperature, clearer-sky day-equivalent proxies, humidity / precipitation comfort, and year-round comfort are covered for 3 representative places.

Uruguay has a humid subtropical climate: mild winters, hot summers, rain in all seasons, and more temperature swings than Mediterranean coastal Europe. Winter is generally mild but can be windy/unstable in the south; summer can be hot, especially inland and north/west. [src-185]

| City | January average high/low | July average high/low | Clearer-sky day-equivalent proxy | Comfort notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Montevideo | ~26.7 / 18.3 C | ~14.4 / 7.2 C | ~217 days/year | Coastal capital; sea breeze moderates summer; rainfall about 1,165 mm/year; winter has cloudy/rainy periods alternating with sunny days. [src-185][src-186] |
| Salto | ~31.7 / 20.0 C | ~17.8 / 8.3 C | ~233 days/year | Warmer inland/north option; more heat exposure in summer; useful if warm winters matter more than coastal moderation. [src-185][src-186] |
| Tacuarembo | ~29.4 / 18.3 C | ~16.7 / 6.7 C | ~223 days/year | Interior climate between Montevideo and Salto; warmer summer than the coast, cooler nights in winter. [src-186] |

**Sunny/clear-day method**: WeatherSpark reports monthly percentages of time in "clear, mostly clear, or partly cloudy" categories. Converted with month lengths, this gives medium-confidence clearer-sky day-equivalent proxies: Montevideo ~217, Salto ~233, Tacuarembo ~223. These are **not official sunny-day counts**. [src-186]

**Household comfort verdict**: climate fit is promising for a couple avoiding long cold winters. The main caveats are humidity/rain throughout the year, windy unstable southern winters, and hot inland/northern summers. Montevideo / Ciudad de la Costa / Maldonado should feel milder than inland Uruguay; Salto is warmer but more heat-exposed. [src-185][src-186]

## Block 5 - Practical verdict

- **Can relocate now**: likely yes for entry and initial filing, because Ukrainian ordinary passports are listed as visa-free and Uruguay has in-country residence routes. [src-180][src-181]
- **Best legalization path for the man**: permanent legal residence using foreign-company employee or independent-worker proof; DN/provisional identity only as a short bridge. [src-182][src-183]
- **Best legalization path for the woman**: separate ordinary residence file tied to family/spouse evidence if married, or independent ordinary residence evidence; do not rely on the DN bridge as a dependent route. [src-182][src-184]
- **Does marriage change the picture**: yes for documentary clarity and possibly the family-constituted 3-year legal-citizenship timeline, but residence still needs a Uruguayan file and means/support evidence. [src-182][src-184]
- **Realism of staying after 03.2027**: medium-positive if ordinary residence is filed early and habitual presence is maintained; not dependent on EU TP extension.

**Pros**:
- Visa-free entry for Ukrainians in the current official admission table. [src-180]
- Permanent-residence route explicitly recognizes foreign-company employment and independent-worker evidence. [src-182]
- Legal citizenship timeline can be 3 years with family constituted in Uruguay or 5 years without family. [src-184]
- Mild winters relative to Central/Eastern Europe. [src-185][src-186]

**Cons / risks**:
- DN/provisional identity is only 6+6 months and should not be treated as the long-term route. [src-183]
- Official page uses "sufficient for maintenance" rather than a fixed remote-income threshold; approval practice needs notary/lawyer verification. [src-182]
- Citizenship requires habitual residence, Spanish ability, and no absences over 6 consecutive months. [src-184]
- Distance from Ukraine/Europe and cost/tax/rent not yet researched.
