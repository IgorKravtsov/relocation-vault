## 1. Executive Summary

Greece is a strong candidate for a Ukrainian couple with one remote IT income ($3000/mo): warm Mediterranean climate, EU/Schengen membership, a functioning digital nomad visa, the 50% tax reduction program for new residents, relatively affordable housing outside Athens, and a clear legal pathway from temporary protection to regular residence status.

**Key concern:** The digital nomad visa requires proof of income that may exceed the couple's actual budget, and the tax regime details need precise verification with a Greek tax accountant.

## 2. Legalization Pathways

### 2.1 Temporary Protection for Ukrainians

**Status:** Available. Extended to **4 March 2027** per Council Implementing Decision 2025/1460. [Confidence: **High** — Source: Greek Ministry of Migration, direct page visit, May 2026]

**Key finding from Greek Ministry (direct source):**
> "Until 04/03/2027, pursuant to Article 194 of Law 5078/2023, as currently in force, holders of temporary protection residence permits are entitled to **apply for any residence permit under the Immigration Code (Law 5038/2023) by submitting an electronic application**, without the need for an entry visa, while a copy of the temporary protection residence permit must be added to the required supporting documents. **The issuance of the new residence permit means resignation from temporary protection.**"
> — Greek Ministry of Migration and Asylum, Information for Displaced Persons from Ukraine, May 2026

**Practical implication:** A Ukrainian can enter Greece, register for temporary protection, and then transition to a digital nomad visa or self-employed residence permit without leaving the country. This is a **major advantage** compared to countries that have not announced post-2027 transitions. [Confidence: **High** for Greece-specific rule; **Medium** for practical execution — needs confirmation of processing times and success rates]

**Resignation from temporary protection** means the couple would give up TP status to take the new residence permit. This is a net positive if the new permit is a longer-term solution.

### 2.2 Digital Nomad Visa (Law 4825/2021, Article 80)

**What it is:** National visa (type D) for remote workers employed by foreign companies or self-employed with foreign clients.

**Requirements (from training knowledge — to be verified with official source):**
- Minimum net income: approximately **€3,500/month** after taxes. [Confidence: **Medium** — widely reported but official figure needs confirmation]
- Work must be performed remotely for a company/client outside Greece.
- Valid for **2 years**, renewable. [Confidence: **Medium**]
- Does NOT automatically lead to permanent residence, but time spent on it likely counts toward the 5-year permanent residence requirement if connected to other permits. [Confidence: **Low** — needs lawyer verification]
- **Family members:** Can be included (spouse, children). For unmarried partners, recognition varies — cohabitation may need to be proven or marriage may be required. [Confidence: **Medium** — marriage is safer]

**Does $3000/mo (€2750) qualify?**
No — if the €3,500/mo requirement is correct, the applicant falls short by ~€750/mo. [Confidence: **Medium** — verify exact threshold]

**Alternatives if DN visa income threshold is too high:**
- Self-employed residence permit (under Immigration Code) — lower income thresholds may apply
- Temporary protection initially, then transition to self-employed permit
- Marriage (if they marry) allows family reunification under the DN visa or other permits

### 2.3 Self-Employed / Freelancer Residence Permit

Greece offers residence permits for self-employed economic activity. A Ukrainian IT worker registering as self-employed ("autonomous professional" / ελεύθερος επαγγελματίας) with Greek tax authorities may apply for a residence permit on this basis. [Confidence: **Medium** — general provision exists, specifics need lawyer]

Minimum income thresholds for self-employed permits: not well documented online. Likely tied to ability to support oneself and pay social contributions. [Confidence: **Low**]

### 2.4 Family Member / Dependent Status

- **Married couples:** Straightforward. Spouse of digital nomad visa holder can get a dependent residence permit (family reunification under Law 5038/2023). [Confidence: **Medium-High**]
- **Unmarried couples:** Greek law does not universally recognize cohabitation for non-EU family reunification. A registered partnership or marriage is safer. [Confidence: **Medium** — verify with migration lawyer]
- The non-working partner's lack of income **does not block** dependent status if the primary applicant meets the income threshold. [Confidence: **Medium**]

### 2.5 Polish Karta Pobytu Impact

**Open question.** The Temporary Protection Directive generally limits TP to one country. However, the Greek Ministry page states that holders of TP residence permits can apply for other residence permits. It is unclear whether someone with a Polish *karta pobytu* (which is a standard residence permit, NOT temporary protection) would be treated differently. [Confidence: **Low** — this is a critical question for a Greek immigration lawyer]

Our working hypothesis: If the Polish permit is a standard residence permit (not TP), the person could potentially apply for TP in Greece, but this is legally uncertain. Alternatively, they could move to Greece and apply directly for a digital nomad visa or self-employed permit. [Confidence: **Low** — needs lawyer verification]

## 3. Taxes for Remote IT Worker

### 3.1 Digital Nomad / New Resident 50% Tax Reduction (Law 4712/2020, amended by Law 4921/2022)

**Key benefit:** 50% reduction on employment / self-employment income for up to **7 years** for individuals who transfer their tax residence to Greece and work remotely for foreign employers/clients. [Confidence: **Medium-High** — program exists and has been extended/amended]

**Qualifying conditions (widely reported, needs official verification):**
- Not been a Greek tax resident for 5 of the last 6 years (or 7 of 8 years depending on version). [Confidence: **Medium**]
- Must transfer tax residence from abroad to Greece. [Confidence: **High**]
- Income must be from employment or self-employment for a foreign company/client. [Confidence: **High**]
- Originally required origin from EU/EEA, but Law 4921/2022 expanded this to include individuals from **any country** with whom Greece has a tax treaty. Ukraine and Greece have a tax treaty (since 1998). [Confidence: **Medium-High** — this is the key point for our case]

**Effective tax burden with 50% reduction on €36,000/year gross:**

Assuming 2024-2025 Greek income tax brackets:

| Bracket (annual) | Rate | Tax on bracket |
|------------------|------|----------------|
| Up to €10,000 | 9% | €900 |
| €10,001–€20,000 | 22% | €2,200 |
| €20,001–€30,000 | 28% | €2,800 |
| €30,001–€40,000 | 36% | €2,160 |

Before reduction: €36,000 taxable → ~€8,060 tax → ~22.4% effective rate.
After 50% reduction: Taxable = €18,000 → ~€2,780 tax → ~7.7% effective rate.

But wait — with the 50% reduction, the method varies:
- Some interpret it as "taxable income is halved," so €36K becomes €18K taxable.
- Others interpret it as "tax owed is halved."
Need exact calculation method. [Confidence: **Low-Medium** for the exact computation — **verify with Greek tax accountant**]

**Social security (EFKA) for self-employed:**
- Approximately **20-27%** of declared income, depending on profession category. [Confidence: **Medium** — needs official EFKA figures]
- For IT workers, likely in the "scientists/liberal professions" category.
- Minimum monthly EFKA contribution: approximately €200-300. [Confidence: **Medium**]

**Estimated total monthly tax + social burden on €3,000/mo ($36K/year):**
- If 50% tax reduction applies: ~€400-600/month (income tax + EFKA). [Confidence: **Low** — approximate estimate]
- This leaves ~€2,400-2,600 net. Very comfortable for Greece.

### 3.2 Without the 50% Reduction

If the applicant does NOT qualify for the 50% reduction (e.g., was Greek tax resident in recent years — not applicable here):
- On €36,000/year: approximately €8,000-9,000 total tax + EFKA per year.
- ~€700-750/month. Still leaves ~€2,250 net.

## 5. Student Status for the Female Partner

### 5.1 Continuing Ukrainian University Remotely
- She can legally reside in Greece as a dependent family member of the working partner once he has a residence permit. [Confidence: **Medium**]
- Distance learning from a Ukrainian university is not a basis for a student residence permit in Greece. [Confidence: **Medium**]

### 5.2 Enrolling in a Greek University
- Greek universities charge low or no tuition for EU citizens. For non-EU students (including Ukrainians with TP), fees vary by program. [Confidence: **Medium**]
- Some programs offer English-language instruction, especially at master's level. Undergraduate is mostly Greek.
- Student residence permit allows work up to 20 hours/week. [Confidence: **Medium**]

### 5.3 No Immediate Need to Transfer
If the couple secures TP or the man's residence permit includes her as dependent, she does not need a separate student visa to live in Greece while studying remotely in Ukraine.

## 6. Climate

**Assessment: Excellent fit for the couple's preference.**

- Athens/Crete: Hot dry summers (25-35°C), very mild winters (8-15°C), ~300 sunny days/year.
- Thessaloniki/Patras: Similar but slightly cooler and wetter in winter.
- Inland (Larissa): Hotter summers, colder winters — less ideal.
- Rain is rare in summer; mild humidity.

[Confidence: **High** — well-documented Mediterranean climate]

## 7. Healthcare

- **Public healthcare (ESY):** Available to TP holders. For residence permit holders, access depends on EFKA contributions (if self-employed) or private insurance.
- **Private insurance:** Required for digital nomad visa. Estimated €50-100/mo per person for basic coverage. €100-200/mo for comprehensive. [Confidence: **Medium**]
- **Quality:** Public hospitals vary by region; Athens has the best facilities. Private care is generally good and affordable compared to Western Europe.
- **For pregnancy/children:** Public system covers maternity. Private insurance recommended for comfort. [Confidence: **Medium**]

## 8. Education (Future Children)

- Public schools: Free, Greek-language. Integration support available for foreign children.
- International schools: Exist in Athens (expensive, €8,000-15,000/year). [Confidence: **Medium**]
- Quality of Greek public education: Mixed reviews; Athens schools are better resourced.
