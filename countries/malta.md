### 5.1. Legalization {status: partial, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-05-26, dod: partial}

> **DoD gap**: first-pass official sources cover TP legal basis, Nomad Residence Permit eligibility / checklist / fees, long-term residence, and naturalisation. This section remains partial because Malta-specific 2027 TP transition rules, detailed TP filing mechanics, and unmarried partner treatment under the NRP need follow-up.

#### Now (until 03.2027)

**Temporary protection.** Malta is bound by the EU temporary-protection framework, and the EU extension source gives the current planning horizon of 04 March 2027 [src-002]. Malta's legal framework includes the *Temporary Protection for Displaced Persons (Minimum Standards) Regulations* under S.L. 420.5 [src-046]. This supports the baseline that Malta can host eligible Ukrainians under temporary protection, but this first pass did not capture a current Malta International Protection Agency page with the exact 2026 application steps because the obvious official pages were WAF-blocked during extraction. Confidence: medium-high for legal basis / EU horizon, low for local procedure details.

**Nomad Residence Permit (NRP).** The Malta NRP is the main official remote-work route. Applicants must be third-country nationals, work remotely using telecoms, and fit one of three foreign-income categories: foreign employer contract, business activity for a foreign company in which the applicant is a partner/shareholder, or freelance / consulting contracts with clients whose permanent establishments are abroad [src-047]. Persons contracted by a foreign company to serve its Maltese subsidiary, or otherwise providing services to Malta-based companies / individuals, are not eligible [src-047][src-049].

The income threshold is high for this couple: Residency Malta requires minimum gross yearly income of **EUR 42,000**, i.e. **EUR 3,500/month gross** (claim-malta-002). That is above the couple's current approximate USD 3,000/month, before exchange-rate and gross/net differences. The route is therefore not realistic unless income rises or the Agency accepts documented gross income above the threshold [src-047].

**NRP documents and procedure.** Applications are submitted through the Residency Malta online portal [src-048]. The new-application checklist requires Form N4 for each applicant, letter of intent, full passport copy, CV, three months of official bank statements with income credited directly to the main applicant's account, police conduct certificates for adults, employment / self-employment / freelance proof, and relationship documents where dependants are included [src-048]. Non-English police certificates and documents need English translation; accommodation and Malta/international health insurance are submitted after Approval in Principle and must cover the permit period [src-048]. Fees are EUR 300 non-refundable application fee per person plus EUR 100 residence-card issuance fee per person, plus visa fees if applicable [src-049]. Processing is expected to take 30 working days after funds are received [src-049].

**Partner / dependent handling under NRP.** The FAQ states that family members can be included and lists spouse, minor children, principally dependent unmarried adult children, and adult children unable to cope independently due to medical condition or disability [src-049]. The checklist separately includes a **de facto partnership** route for unmarried couples in a stable relationship for more than two consecutive years, supported by a declaration, dated photographs, and two evidence types such as utility bills, leases, bank statements / joint accounts, flight tickets, or affidavits [src-048]. Operational baseline: marriage is cleaner; if unmarried, rely on de facto partner inclusion only after written programme confirmation (claim-malta-004).

#### After 4 March 2027

Verification pass baseline: no Malta-specific official bridge from Ukrainian temporary protection into an ordinary Maltese residence permit has been captured. The safe planning baseline is: do not rely on TP after 04 March 2027 unless Malta publishes a transition rule; plan an ordinary route before expiry [src-002][src-046]. The NRP can be a fallback only if the income threshold is met, but it is capped and explicitly not a PR/citizenship path [src-049].

#### Residence without local employer

NRP structurally supports foreign-client / foreign-employer remote work and freelancing, but excludes services to Malta-based companies or individuals [src-047][src-049]. For the male IT worker, this is a conceptual fit only if income rises above EUR 42,000/year gross and contracts / bank statements can document at least five cumulative months of guaranteed income from the application date [src-047][src-048].

#### PR and citizenship

Identità states that third-country nationals may apply for long-term residence after legally and continuously residing in Malta for five years or more, with stable and regular resources, own accommodation, and integration measures; the long-term-residence status is permanent and the card is issued for five years; fee EUR 500 [src-050]. However, Residency Malta's NRP FAQ explicitly says the NRP **does not lead to any sort of permanent / long-term residency or citizenship** and is separate from the Malta Permanent Residence Programme [src-049].

For citizenship by ordinary residence, Komunità Malta states that adults may apply if they resided in Malta throughout the 12 months immediately before application and for an aggregate minimum of four years during the preceding six years, have good character, adequate Maltese or English knowledge, and are suitable citizens; the Minister has discretion to grant or refuse [src-051]. Malta accepts dual / multiple citizenship, but applicants must check the other country's law [src-051]. Because the NRP is explicitly non-converting, the citizenship route needs a different countable residence basis before it can be treated as realistic for this couple.

#### Polish karta pobytu interaction

A Polish residence title does not substitute for a Maltese status. If the Polish status is temporary protection, the EU one-Member-State TP principle remains the safe baseline from the EU temporary-protection source [src-002]. If it is an ordinary Polish residence permit, it may help Schengen logistics but does not itself authorize long-term residence or work in Malta; Malta requires its own residence basis [src-047][src-050].

#### Personal playbook for our couple

1. **Do not treat Malta as a stable post-2027 plan yet.** TP may help short-term if eligible, but the bridge after 04 March 2027 is not captured.
2. **NRP is currently income-blocked.** Raise documented gross remote income to at least EUR 42,000/year before relying on Malta.
3. **If pursuing NRP**, prepare passport scans, CV, police certificates, English translations, three months of bank statements, foreign client / employer contracts, and a one-year accommodation / health-insurance plan [src-048].
4. **Relationship strategy**: marriage is the lower-risk route for inclusion. If not married, collect two years of de facto partnership evidence and seek written confirmation before filing [src-048][src-049].
5. **Long-term strategy**: Malta likely needs a separate countable residence route beyond NRP for PR/citizenship; research taxes, ordinary work/self-employment permits, and MPRP later before assigning a tier.

### 5.2. Climate {status: deep, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-05-30, dod: passed}

Malta has a warm Mediterranean climate with mild winters, dry sunny summers, and autumn rain concentration. Climates to Travel reports Valletta average temperatures from about 12.5 °C in February to 27.5 °C in August, rainfall around 600 mm/year, and about 3,000 annual sunshine hours, making Malta one of Europe's sunniest places [src-052]. The Valletta table gives January min/max about 50/61 °F (10/16 °C), July about 72/90 °F (22/32 °C), annual humidity about 72%, 58 precipitation days/year, and 3,055 sunshine hours [src-052].

For practical relocation comfort, Malta is very favorable against the couple's cold-winter constraint: freezing weather and snow are extremely rare, sea swimming is usually realistic July-October, and winter still has sunny periods [src-052]. Main downsides are humid winters, windy storm periods, and Saharan sirocco / heat waves that can push temperatures above 40 °C in exceptional summer events [src-052].

Because Malta is geographically compact, the first pass used Valletta as the island-level climate anchor; WeatherSpark now adds city-level clearer-sky proxies for Valletta, Sliema, and Victoria/Gozo. Converting monthly percentages for clear / mostly clear / partly cloudy conditions gives **Valletta ~266**, **Sliema ~266**, and **Victoria ~265** clearer-sky day-equivalents/year [src-120]. This is a medium-confidence proxy rather than an official meteorological sunny-day count, but it is enough for practical climate screening and closes `vq-018`.

### 5.3. Taxes {status: partial, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-06-06, dod: partial}

> **DoD status**: Partial. This pass captures tax residence / source-remittance scope, 2026 PIT bands, NRP authorized-work taxation, self-employed social-security and VAT baselines, filing mechanics, marriage effects, and first USD 3,000/month worked examples. Missing before passing DoD: official / adviser confirmation for how a Ukrainian foreign-client IT worker on the NRP is classified for Maltese source, remittance, social-security, and VAT purposes.

#### Tax residence and scope

PwC's Malta summary says a person who is both ordinarily resident and domiciled in Malta is taxed on worldwide income. A person who is ordinarily resident but not domiciled in Malta is generally taxed on Malta-source income and foreign income remitted to Malta, but not on unremitted foreign income or foreign capital gains [src-311][src-312]. A non-resident is taxed only on Malta-source income [src-311]. Malta has few bright-line residence rules; ordinary residence is a facts-and-circumstances concept tied to living there in the ordinary course of life, and remittance is also factual [src-312].

For this couple, the safe assumption is that living in Malta as a real base requires Maltese tax advice. The key classification question is whether the IT worker's foreign-client work performed while physically in Malta is treated as NRP "authorised work" and / or Malta-source income, not merely foreign income left offshore.

#### Nomad Residence Permit tax treatment

PwC records specific Maltese income-tax rules for third-country nationals holding a valid Nomad Residence Permit. The main applicant is subject to Maltese income tax at a flat **10%** rate on income from "authorised work", before double-tax relief if applicable; that income is treated as the first part of taxable income, and generally the main applicant is not subject to Maltese income tax on authorised-work income in the first year [src-311]. Malta's Legal Notice 277 of 2023 is the official legislative anchor for the Nomad Residence Permits (Income Tax) Rules, 2023, but this pass used the Legislation Malta page only as a legal-notice metadata capture, not a full rule-text extraction [src-315].

This looks attractive, but it does **not** solve the couple's current Malta blocker. Residency Malta's NRP threshold remains EUR 42,000/year gross, while USD 3,000/month is only about EUR 31,152/year at the run-date FX rate [src-047][src-293]. Therefore the 10% NRP tax regime is useful only if gross remote income rises enough to qualify for the permit.

#### Ordinary resident / self-employed fallback baseline

If the worker is not taxed under the NRP authorised-work rules, ordinary Maltese resident PIT uses progressive 2026 rates. The single bands are 0% up to EUR 12,000, 15% to EUR 16,000, 25% to EUR 60,000, and 35% above EUR 60,000; the married computation is more favorable, with 0% up to EUR 15,500, 15% to EUR 23,000, then 25% to EUR 60,000 [src-311]. PwC also states that no personal allowances by deduction or credit are granted, but business-related deductions may be available for persons exercising a trade, business, profession, or vocation [src-314].

For self-occupied / self-employed individuals, PwC gives social-security contributions of 15% of annual net income earned in the previous year, capped at EUR 83.89/week for persons born on or after 1 January 1962 in calendar year 2026 [src-313]. That cap is about EUR 4,362/year. PwC's VAT summary says Malta's standard VAT rate is 18%; service businesses with annual turnover not higher than EUR 35,000 may qualify as small enterprises, in which case they do not charge VAT but still usually have a registration requirement unless making exempt-without-credit supplies [src-313].

#### Filing and payment mechanics

Tax returns are calendar-year based and normally due by the end of June of the year following the basis year [src-314]. Married spouses, and civil-union partners, are jointly responsible for filing and choose a responsible spouse; a separate computation can be elected for earned income, so spouses / civil-union partners can be assessed separately at single or parent rates for earned income [src-314]. Self-employed taxpayers outside the Final Settlement System generally make provisional tax payments in three instalments during the basis year: 20%, 30%, and 50% based on the last self-assessment [src-314].

#### Worked examples for USD 3,000/month

Run-date FX source `src-293` gives 1 USD = EUR 0.865342, so USD 3,000/month is about EUR 2,596/month or EUR 31,152/year.

| Scenario | PIT / contributions | Estimated monthly net |
|---|---:|---:|
| NRP authorised work, first year relief concept | 0% Malta PIT on authorised work in first year; social-security/VAT not confirmed | about EUR 2,596 before any non-Malta taxes / contributions |
| NRP authorised work after first year | 10% PIT on authorised work; social-security/VAT not confirmed | about EUR 2,336 before any social-security / VAT edge cases |
| Ordinary self-employed, single rates + capped 2026 social security | PIT about EUR 4,388/year; social security about EUR 4,362/year | about EUR 1,867 |
| Ordinary self-employed, married computation + capped 2026 social security | PIT about EUR 3,238/year; social security about EUR 4,362/year | about EUR 1,963 |

The NRP examples are strategically limited because USD 3,000/month does not meet the NRP income threshold. The ordinary examples are conservative first-pass stress tests: they assume no special deduction beyond capped social security, no optimized company structure, and no final answer on VAT / source-remittance treatment.

#### Marriage / partner tax effects

Marriage or civil union can matter for Malta tax computation: spouses / civil-union partners file within the responsible-spouse system, and earned income can be separately computed; the married bands are more favorable for a one-income couple than single bands in this income range [src-311][src-314]. This is a tax benefit separate from immigration: for the NRP dependent file, marriage is still cleaner than relying on de facto partnership evidence.

#### Practical tax playbook

1. Do not use Malta as a tax-positive relocation case unless the NRP income threshold is first solved; current income is below EUR 42,000/year gross.
2. If income rises above threshold, ask a Maltese tax adviser to confirm that the exact foreign-client IT contracts are "authorised work" under the NRP rules and how first-year relief / 10% tax applies.
3. Confirm whether Maltese Class Two / self-occupied social-security contributions apply to an NRP remote worker and whether any foreign social-security certificate or treaty position changes that result.
4. If not using NRP, model ordinary self-employment at roughly EUR 1,867-1,963/month net before accountant/VAT edge cases at the current income level.
5. For marriage planning, treat Malta as one of the countries where marriage / civil union can improve both immigration dependency clarity and tax computation, but do not marry for tax alone without immigration counsel.
