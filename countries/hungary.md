#### Now (until 03.2027)

**Temporary protection for Ukrainians.** Hungary can grant temporary protection to Ukrainian nationals who were residing in Ukraine before 24 February 2022, protected non-Ukrainian residents of Ukraine, and qualifying family members. The application is treated as submitted after identity verification, personal-data recording, and biometrics. [src-092] The EU-level temporary-protection horizon in the vault remains 04 March 2027. [src-002]

A TP applicant/beneficiary can work in Hungary without a specific work permit, including through a temporary-work agency, and can access listed healthcare categories if not otherwise socially insured: primary care, urgent specialist/hospital care, broader specialist/hospital care for vulnerable persons, prenatal and obstetric care, public-health medicines, oncology/chronic care, emergency dental care, and maternity-care services. [src-092]

**White Card / digital nomad.** Hungary's White Card is the cleanest remote-work route captured in this first pass. It is for a third-country national who either has a verified employment relationship outside Hungary and performs work from Hungary using advanced digital technology, or owns a share in a profitable company outside Hungary and performs work/manages that company from Hungary. It is explicitly not for a person who pursues gainful activity in Hungary or holds a share in a Hungarian company. [src-093]

The key budget problem is the means test: OIF states that sufficient resources are met if the applicant's monthly legal income is at least EUR 3,000 net for at least 6 months before entry, and the applicant must keep that income throughout the stay. This is above the couple's approximate USD 3,000/month gross/current-income assumption, so the route is not safely budget-compatible unless income rises or the exact net/gross evidence accepted by the consulate is verified. [src-093]

Procedurally, if the applicant lacks a Hungarian residence permit and entry is not visa-exempt for the intended filing route, the issuance application is submitted outside Hungary at a Hungarian diplomatic/consular mission in the country of nationality or habitual residence. Extensions are filed in Hungary through Enter Hungary, can be made only once for the same purpose, and require holding a valid permit and having resided in Hungary at least 90 days in any 180-day period before application. OIF gives a 21-day procedural administration period. [src-093]

**Guest self-employment / entrepreneur route.** Hungary also has a residence permit for guest self-employment. For a registered self-employed person, OIF lists evidence that annual income from self-employment exceeds the current minimum wage 24 times plus a no-tax/no-contribution-debt certificate. For a business-organisation chief executive, the page requires either at least five Hungarian/free-movement full-time employees for six continuous months, or evidence that the applicant's residence is essential and likely to generate enough revenue under a detailed business-activity declaration. [src-094]

For this couple, guest self-employment may be a fallback if the man can build a Hungarian self-employed/business structure, but it is not yet a simple digital-nomad substitute. The evidence burden and tax-registration path require a later tax/business deep dive. The permit is initially up to 1 year and can be extended for the same purpose for up to 2 more years, but total validity cannot exceed 3 years from first issuance. [src-094]

#### After 4 March 2027

No Hungary-specific official bridge from Ukrainian temporary protection into an ordinary residence permit after 04 March 2027 is captured. The operational baseline is now closed for planning purposes: do not rely on TP alone after the EU horizon; plan to hold or qualify for an ordinary Hungarian status (guest self-employment, family, study, work, or another route) before TP expiry unless Hungary later publishes a transition rule. [src-002][src-092]

#### Residence without local employer

- **White Card**: best fit for foreign remote employment/foreign company ownership, but requires EUR 3,000 net monthly income and excludes Hungarian gainful activity or a Hungarian company share. [src-093]
- **Guest self-employment**: possible local self-employment/business route, but the captured official page ties eligibility to self-employment income above 24 times the minimum wage annually or a business-organisation evidence package. [src-094]
- **Student route for the woman**: not researched in this pass; a Ukrainian distance-learning program alone should not be assumed to create a Hungarian student residence basis. [verification required]

#### Family reunification / partner handling

OIF's family-reunification page supports a spouse/registered-partner/minor-child baseline rather than an unmarried-partner baseline. It defines family member to include a spouse, including a registered partner under Act XXIX of 2009, minor children, and limited dependent parent/sibling/ascendant categories; it does not list an unmarried partner. More importantly for this couple, OIF explicitly says family reunification may not be granted if the sponsor is a holder of a White Card. Operational baseline: a White Card cannot be used to sponsor the woman; if Hungary is chosen, she needs TP/independent eligibility or the man needs a different sponsor-eligible status. [src-095]

Family-reunification applicants must show subsistence, accommodation, return/onward-travel capacity, and healthcare coverage; evidence can include bank statements/savings, employer/tax-authority income documents, or a notarised maintenance declaration by a family member with evidence of ability to maintain the applicant. General validity is up to 3 years and may be extended by up to 3 years, but it cannot exceed the sponsor's permit or the applicant's travel-document validity. [src-095]

#### Polish karta pobytu interaction

A Polish residence card does not substitute for Hungarian residence authorization. If the existing Polish status is temporary protection, the EU one-Member-State TP planning baseline from earlier runs still applies; if it is a different Polish residence permit, it may help prove lawful residence/habitual residence for consular filing but does not itself create a right to long-stay residence in Hungary. [src-002][src-093]

#### PR and citizenship

Hungary's National Residence Card is the captured long-term residence anchor. OIF states that third-country nationals holding a residence permit, interim permanent residence permit, or interim residence card may be eligible if they have resided legally and without interruption in Hungary for at least 3 years before application, subject to Hungary's interests and additional conditions. The card is valid up to 10 years and may be extended by 10 years. [src-096]

Citizenship conditions were not official-primary verified in this pass. Treat naturalization timeline, language/civics requirements, and whether White Card or guest self-employment residence counts toward every long-term status as [verification required] until a dedicated PR/citizenship pass.

#### Personal playbook for our couple

1. **Do not rely on Hungary TP as the only post-2027 plan.** TP can support immediate safety/status if eligible, but no captured Hungarian bridge exists. [src-002][src-092]
2. **White Card only works if income is raised/structured above EUR 3,000 net/month for 6 months.** Current USD 3,000/month is likely insufficient, especially if it is gross and must support two people. [src-093]
3. **If choosing Hungary anyway, investigate guest self-employment plus tax registration next.** It may fit an IT freelancer better long-term than the White Card, but the official criteria are evidence-heavy and capped at 3 years before a new application. [src-094]
4. **White Card does not solve partner dependency.** OIF excludes White Card holders as family-reunification sponsors, so the woman needs TP/independent eligibility, or the man must use a different sponsor-eligible Hungarian status before family reunification is a viable plan. [src-095]
5. **Use the Polish karta pobytu only as supporting documentation, not as Hungarian status.** It does not replace a Hungarian permit.

### 5.2. Climate {status: partial, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-05-28, dod: partial}

Hungary has a continental Central European climate rather than a Mediterranean one. Winters are meaningfully colder than in Spain/Portugal/Greece/Cyprus, while summers are warm to hot. For Budapest, the 1991–2020 table gives a January daily mean around 0.0°C and July daily mean around 22.5°C, with average relative humidity around 68% and about 1,928 annual sunshine hours. [src-097]

Debrecen is colder in winter and similarly hot in summer: January daily mean about -0.8°C, July about 21.9°C, annual relative humidity about 72.6%, and about 2,043 annual sunshine hours. [src-098] Pécs in the southwest is one of the more comfortable warmer options: January daily mean about 0.4°C, July/August around 22.0°C daily mean, annual relative humidity about 71.3%, and about 2,058 annual sunshine hours. [src-099]

**Sunny-day blocker**: the captured sources give sunshine hours, humidity, and temperature normals but not direct annual sunny/clear-day counts. Keep §5.2 partial and close after a direct sunny/clear-day source is found for Budapest, Debrecen, and Pécs. [verification required]

## Block 5 — Practical verdict

- **Can relocate now**: Possible, but Hungary is not yet a top-fit first-pass route. TP may work if eligible; White Card requires EUR 3,000 net/month, excludes local work, and cannot sponsor family reunification; guest self-employment needs deeper business/tax validation. [src-092][src-093][src-094][src-095]
- **Best legalization path for the man**: White Card only if income is safely above EUR 3,000 net/month and the woman has her own status; otherwise investigate guest self-employment or another sponsor-eligible route.
- **Best legalization path for the woman**: Temporary protection if independently eligible, or independent study/work/other status; family reunification is not available from a White Card sponsor, and unmarried dependency is not captured. [src-095]
- **Does marriage change the picture**: It may help only if the sponsor holds a sponsor-eligible Hungarian status; marriage alone does not fix White Card family-reunification exclusion.
- **Realism of staying after 03.2027**: Unclear/medium-low until ordinary route feasibility is verified; no captured TP bridge.

**Pros**:
- Official White Card exists for foreign remote work. [src-093]
- National Residence Card may be available after 3 years of legal uninterrupted residence for eligible permit holders. [src-096]
- Budapest/Pécs/Debrecen have relatively sunny Central European climates by regional standards. [src-097][src-098][src-099]

**Cons / risks**:
- White Card EUR 3,000 net/month requirement is above the couple's current budget assumption. [src-093]
- White Card holders cannot sponsor family reunification, so the partner needs independent eligibility or the man needs a different sponsor-eligible status. [src-095]
