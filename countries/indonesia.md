## Block 3 — Profile by section

### 5.1. Legalization {status: partial, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-06-05, dod: partial}

> **DoD status**: This first pass opens entry and the main remote-work visa. It does not yet pass DoD because dependent mechanics, PR/citizenship counting, tax residence, and alternative residence categories for a US$36,000/year foreign-client IT profile remain unresolved.

#### Now (until 03.2027)

- **Short scouting entry**: Indonesia's official immigration country list for VoA / visa-free / calling-visa subjects lists `Ukraina` and `Polandia`; the B1 visit visa on arrival allows up to 30 days, extendable once to a total of 60 days, with a Rp500,000 fee for the first 30 days. B1 holders may not sell goods/services or receive wages or similar compensation from Indonesian persons or corporations. [src-263][src-264]
- **Ukrainian TP context**: Indonesia is not an EU temporary-protection jurisdiction; March 2027 is therefore not an Indonesian TP deadline. The relevant question is whether the couple can secure an ordinary Indonesian status before relying on Indonesia as a base.
- **Polish karta pobytu**: Not captured as giving any Indonesian long-stay right. Use it only as an existing third-country residence/travel document where forms ask for residence history; it does not replace an Indonesian visa/residence permit. [verification required]

#### After 4 March 2027

- No Indonesia-specific Ukraine temporary-protection bridge is captured, and none should be assumed. For this vault, Indonesia planning must be ordinary-route planning: visitor entry for scouting, then a limited-stay status if the requirements fit.

#### Residence without local employer

- **E33G Visa Rumah Kedua Pekerja Jarak Jauh / remote-worker limited-stay visa**: the official Indonesian immigration page says it allows residence in Indonesia to perform duties for a company outside Indonesia, is a limited-stay visa with multiple entry/re-entry while the re-entry permit is valid, and allows a 1-year stay. The limited stay and re-entry permit are issued automatically after admission, and extension can be handled online through `evisa.imigrasi.go.id`. [src-264]
- **Key E33G requirements captured**: passport valid at least 6 months; three-month bank statement for living costs of at least US$2,000 or equivalent; recent photo; CV; travel itinerary; a bank account proving salary/income of at least US$60,000 per year; and an employment agreement with a company established outside Indonesia. Processing is stated as five working days after visa payment. [src-264]
- **Budget fit**: the US$60,000/year requirement is materially above the couple's current ~US$36,000/year gross profile. Unless income rises, E33G is not a realistic primary route. [src-264]
- **Fees captured**: the E33G one-year stay package is Rp7,000,000, broken into Rp500,000 limited-stay visa, Rp2,000,000 category-II verification, Rp3,000,000 limited-stay permit up to 1 year, and Rp1,500,000 re-entry permit up to 1 year. [src-264]

#### PR and citizenship

- Not captured in this pass. The E33G page says the stay permit can be extended and can be converted to another stay permit, but that sentence alone is not enough to treat E33G as a PR/citizenship ladder. Queue follow-up before any settlement scoring.

#### Personal playbook for our couple

1. Treat Indonesia as a **scouting / possible bridge** country, not a proven settlement country yet.
2. Use B1 VoA/e-VoA only for a short reconnaissance trip; do not work for Indonesian clients or employers on visitor status. [src-263]
3. Do not rely on E33G at current income: the official income proof is US$60,000/year, above the current ~US$36,000/year. [src-264]
4. If income rises above US$60,000/year, verify the serving e-visa workflow, dependent/spouse mechanics, tax residence consequences, and whether repeated E33G years can ever lead to ITAP/PR before committing.

### 5.2. Climate {status: deep, depth: 2, last_updated: 2026-06-05, dod: passed}

Indonesia has no cold-winter problem: the country is generally equatorial/tropical, hot, humid, and rainy, with plains commonly around 22–25 °C nights and 30–32 °C days. The main comfort risk is not cold but year-round humidity, monsoon rain, flooding in some cities, and limited clear-sky periods. [src-265]

| City / area | January baseline | July baseline | Rain / humidity / comfort | Sunshine / clearer-sky proxy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Jakarta | about 75–87 °F | about 74–90 °F | Hot/muggy; rainy season October-May; January-February flood risk; about 70.7 inches / 125 rain days per year; average humidity around 77–78% in the wettest months. | Climate to Travel gives ~2,975 sunshine hours/year; WeatherSpark clearer-sky proxy computes to ~77 day-equivalent days/year. [src-265][src-266] |
| Surabaya | about 76–90 °F | about 73–88 °F | Hot/muggy; rainy season October-May, drier June-September; about 68.9 inches / 147 rain days per year; January humidity around 81%. | Climate to Travel gives ~2,190 sunshine hours/year; WeatherSpark proxy computes to ~96 clearer-sky day-equivalent days/year. [src-265][src-266] |
| Bandung | about 68–83 °F | about 65–84 °F | Highland Java is cooler and may be more livable for heat-sensitive residents, but still rainy: about 72.4 inches / 156 rain days per year, with flood-prone wet months. | Climate to Travel gives ~2,465 sunshine hours/year. [src-265] |
| Bali / Denpasar | about 77–87 °F | about 75–85 °F | Tropical, humid, and rainy November-March; southern Bali is less rainy than the interior, with a clearer dry season April-October and about 67.5 inches / 126 rain days per year. | Climate to Travel gives ~2,990 sunshine hours/year, with much better sunshine in June-September. [src-265] |

**Climate verdict**: section 5.2 passes at medium confidence. Indonesia is warm enough, but the couple should screen for humidity tolerance, flood exposure, air quality, and whether Bali/Bandung comfort offsets legal-route weakness.
