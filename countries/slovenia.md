### 5.1. Legalization {status: partial, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-05-29, dod: partial}

#### Now (until 03.2027)

**Temporary protection.** Slovenia's government page states that people who left Ukraine due to the Russian military invasion may apply for temporary protection in Slovenia. Eligible groups include Ukrainian citizens, people with international/equivalent protection in Ukraine, qualifying family members, and some third-country nationals/stateless persons with permanent residence in Ukraine who cannot safely return. [src-110]

The procedure starts with police registration after entry, followed by an application to any administrative unit within three working days. The administrative unit issues a certificate while the case is pending; if approved, the temporary-protection card proves identity and is valid as a temporary residence permit in Slovenia for the duration of temporary protection. Applicants are fee-exempt and should register temporary residence after receiving protection. [src-110]

EU temporary protection is extended to **04 March 2027** at EU level. Slovenia's page states that the card is valid for the duration of temporary protection; use the shared EU source for the end-date anchor until a Slovenia-specific 2027 extension notice is captured. [src-002][src-110]

**Digital-nomad temporary residence permit.** Slovenia introduced a temporary residence permit for digital nomads on 21 November 2025. A digital nomad is a non-EU/EEA foreigner who is either employed by, works under a civil-law contract for, or is self-employed for a business entity based outside Slovenia, with work performed remotely via information and communication technologies. The key legal idea is that the person is **not entering the Slovenian labour market**, so labour-market admission through the Employment Service is not required. [src-111]

Applications may be filed at any Slovenian diplomatic mission/consular post abroad, or at any administrative unit in Slovenia if the applicant is already legally residing in Slovenia. The permit may be issued for up to **one year** and cannot be extended. Reapplication is possible six months after expiry. During validity, the holder may apply for another type of temporary residence permit based on a different purpose of stay. [src-111]

The sufficient-means rule is formula-based: monthly funds must be at least **twice the average monthly net salary in Slovenia**, calculated on the basis of the average monthly gross salary most recently published in the Official Gazette. Proof may come from any lawful income sources. The current salary screen is now captured from Slovenia's statistical office: March 2026 average earnings were EUR 2,678.28 gross and EUR 1,678.81 net; applying the twice-net-salary formula gives about **EUR 3,357.62/month**. That is above the couple's current ~$3,000/month nominal income, before any dependent buffer, so the DN route should be treated as budget-failing/tight unless income rises or savings/other lawful income can be counted. [src-111][src-187]

Family treatment is better than in several neighbouring countries: Slovenia says digital nomads may reunite with family members immediately, without restrictions tied to the duration of the foreigner's residence or permit validity. The exact dependent-document checklist and whether an unmarried partner qualifies through the general long-term-relationship category need a later application-prep pass. [src-111][src-113]

**Self-employed / single permit fallback.** If the DN route is too short or the couple wants a more ordinary work/residence basis, Slovenia also has a single permit for work as a self-employed person. EU Immigration Portal guidance says a person who wants to work as self-employed in Slovenia must obtain a single permit and, before engaging in self-employed activities, must generally have resided in Slovenia on a valid residence permit for at least one year, except for persons entered in the business register as a self-employed professional activity. Applications can be filed at a Slovenian diplomatic/consular post abroad or at the competent administrative unit in Slovenia. [src-112]

#### After 4 March 2027

Slovenia has a more concrete safety valve than the countries where only a no-bridge baseline was captured. The government page says that when temporary protection ends due to expiry/cancellation or acquisition of another protected nationality, the person may apply within **10 days** after termination to the competent Slovenian authority for a temporary residence permit for any purpose specified in the law governing entry, exit and stay of aliens. If filed on time, the administrative unit issues a certificate valid as a residence permit until a final decision; if the person was employed, working, or self-employed at TP termination and timely applies for a single permit, the certificate allows residence and work until final decision. [src-110]

Operational baseline: this is not an automatic conversion into a specific long-term status, but it is an official post-TP filing window. For planning, the couple should still qualify for DN, self-employed/single-permit, family, study, or another ordinary route before the TP horizon rather than waiting until the 10-day window. [src-110][src-111][src-112]

#### Residence without local employer

The best no-local-employer route is the new digital-nomad permit if the man can meet the funds formula and prove foreign-client/foreign-company remote work. It is directly designed for foreign remote work and excludes entry into the Slovenian labour market. Its long-term weakness is duration: up to one year, non-extendable, with a six-month gap before reapplication. [src-111]

A self-employed/single-permit route may become relevant after one year of valid residence or if the man qualifies through a business-register/professional-activity exception, but this needs a dedicated business/tax pass. [src-112]

#### PR and citizenship

The EU Immigration Portal states that after **five continuous years** of legal residence on the basis of temporary residence, a person can apply for a permanent residence permit, with absence rules described in the source. For operational planning, do not rely on the one-year non-renewable DN permit as the main PR clock by itself. Use it only as a bridge into another temporary residence purpose; later application-prep should confirm residence-type counting, but the safe strategy is to move to a renewable ordinary permit before depending on the five-year PR route. [src-111][src-112]

Slovenia's government citizenship page states that ordinary naturalisation is possible after living in Slovenia for at least **10 years**, including a continuous period of **five years** of residence before application, subject to other legal conditions. [src-114]

#### Polish `karta pobytu` interaction

Operational baseline: a Polish residence permit does not replace a Slovenian long-stay status. It may help prove lawful EU residence for filing logistics, but Slovenia still requires its own TP card, DN permit, single permit, family permit, or other status. If the Polish status is temporary protection, apply the EU one-Member-State TP rule conservatively and do not assume two active TP files can coexist. [src-002][src-110][src-111]

1. **Current safe layer**: if eligible and not already relying on TP elsewhere, Slovenia TP is an immediate lawful-residence option with a card treated as temporary residence. [src-110]
2. **Best ordinary remote-work candidate**: test the DN permit first because it is explicitly for foreign remote work and can be filed abroad or in Slovenia while legally present. [src-111]
3. **Budget warning**: the captured March 2026 net-earnings screen gives a twice-net-salary figure of about EUR 3,357.62/month, above the current ~$3,000/month income. Treat DN as not safely affordable unless income rises or other accepted lawful funds are documented. [src-111][src-187]
4. **Partner strategy**: use each partner's own TP where possible; for derived residence, check the DN immediate family regime and the general family-reunification category covering spouse, civil partner, civil-union partner, or long-term relationship partner. [src-111][src-113]
5. **Long-term plan**: because DN is non-extendable, use the DN year to prepare a different ordinary permit (single/self-employed, employment, family, study, or other) if Slovenia remains attractive. [src-111][src-112]

### 5.2. Climate {status: deep, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-05-30, dod: passed}

> **DoD status**: passed. Three-city temperature, precipitation, humidity, sunshine-hour baselines, and medium-confidence clearer-sky day-equivalent proxies are captured.

Slovenia is climatically split: Ljubljana and Maribor are continental with cold, humid winters and warm summers, while the short Adriatic coast around Portorož/Koper is much milder and sunnier. For a warm-climate preference, the coast is the relevant subregion; Ljubljana is practical but not especially sunny in winter.

| City | January mean | July mean | Annual sunshine | Comfort notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Ljubljana | 1.2 °C | 21.9 °C | ~1,955 h/year | Humid/foggy winters, abundant precipitation, warm summers with possible heat waves and thunderstorms; January humidity around 87%. [src-115] |
| Maribor | 0.5 °C | 21.4 °C | ~2,045 h/year | Drier than Ljubljana but still cold in winter; warm summers, thunderstorms possible; January humidity around 82%. [src-116] |
| Portorož / coastal Koper area | 5.3 °C | 23.4 °C | ~2,415 h/year | Clearly the warmest and sunniest captured area; mild coastal winters, hot beach-season summers, January humidity around 80%. [src-117] |

WeatherSpark provides monthly percentages for the broader clearer-sky categories (clear, mostly clear, or partly cloudy). Converting those monthly percentages to annual day-equivalent proxies gives **Ljubljana ~183**, **Maribor ~185**, and **Portorož ~203** clearer-sky day-equivalents/year [src-122]. This is a medium-confidence screening proxy rather than an official meteorological sunny-day count, but it closes the §5.2 climate blocker.

- **Best legalization path for the man**: Digital-nomad permit if the twice-average-net-salary formula is met; otherwise TP now plus a researched ordinary permit strategy. [src-111]
- **Best legalization path for the woman**: Her own TP if eligible; otherwise derived family route must be checked under DN/family-reunification rules, with marriage or documented long-term partnership likely important. [src-111][src-113]
- **Does marriage change the picture**: Likely helpful. General family reunification lists spouse, civil partner/civil union partner, and long-term relationship partner; proof standards for unmarried partners still need a checklist. [src-113]
- **Realism of staying after 03.2027**: Medium pending verification. Slovenia has an official post-TP 10-day filing window and a DN route, but DN is non-extendable and the long-term bridge must be built through another ordinary permit. [src-110][src-111]

**Pros**:

## Block 6 — Practical playbook (working relocation guide)

### 6a. Before the move (what to prepare in Ukraine / Poland)
- Confirm whether either partner already holds temporary protection in another EU Member State; do not assume two active TP statuses are possible. [src-002]
- Prepare passports, Ukrainian civil-status documents, proof of remote employment/contracts, invoices/bank statements, health-insurance evidence, and proof of lawful income.
- For DN, collect evidence that all work is for foreign employers/clients or foreign self-employment and is performed remotely via ICT. [src-111]
- Before relying on the partner route, obtain a checklist for marriage/civil-union/long-term-relationship proof under the DN or family-reunification route. [src-111][src-113]

### 6b. First month after arrival
- If using TP, complete police registration, then apply to an administrative unit within three working days and keep the certificate while the case is pending. [src-110]
- Register temporary residence after obtaining temporary protection. [src-110]
- If using DN from inside Slovenia, file while legally present at an administrative unit; if outside Slovenia, file at a Slovenian diplomatic/consular post. [src-111]

### 6c. First 3–6 months
- Recheck the DN threshold immediately before filing; the current captured screen is about EUR 3,357.62/month, so preserve ongoing income proof and do not assume a ~$3,000/month file passes. [src-111][src-187]
- If Slovenia is attractive beyond one year, begin switching-plan work early: self-employed/single permit, employment, family, study or another statutory purpose. [src-111][src-112]
- Track residence continuity if planning for permanent residence after five years. [src-112]

### 6d. Before March 2027 (critical deadline)
- Do not treat TP as the whole plan. Slovenia's official post-TP window is only a short application window; have the ordinary permit strategy validated before the EU horizon. [src-110]
- If employed/working/self-employed at TP termination and timely applying for a single permit, Slovenia says the certificate can allow residence and work until final decision. [src-110]

### 6e. Long-term (3–7+ years)
- Permanent residence baseline: five continuous years of legal residence on temporary residence, subject to counting/absence rules. [src-112]
- Citizenship baseline: ordinary naturalisation after at least 10 years in Slovenia, including five continuous years before application. [src-114]
