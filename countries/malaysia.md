- **Short-stay entry / scouting**: Malaysian Immigration's official visa-requirement page was captured as the primary table target, but Ukraine's exact row/status was not cleanly extracted in the first pass. The MDAC portal includes Ukraine as a nationality option for arrival-card registration, which supports operational arrival-card availability but does not establish visa-free duration. For screening, close this as a travel-logistics issue rather than a relocation blocker: Malaysia planning depends on DE Rantau / another pass, not on visitor stay. Recheck the readable visa table, MYVISA/eVISA, embassy, or TIMATIC before travel. [src-251][src-252]
- **DE Rantau Digital Nomad Pass**: MDEC describes DE Rantau as a Professional Visit Pass for qualified foreign digital nomads. It is valid for 3 to 12 months and renewable for an additional 12 months, for up to 24 months total. It is open to all nationalities except Israel, and applicants do not need to be in Malaysia to apply. [src-253]
- **Income fit**: for Tech Talent/Profession applicants, MDEC lists an annual income requirement of more than USD 24,000; for Non-Tech Talent/Profession, more than USD 60,000. The working partner is in IT and earns about USD 36,000/year, so the tech track is above threshold if the work evidence is accepted. [src-253]
- **Dependents**: MDEC says the pass can bring spouse and child/children, plus parent(s) for the main pass holder. Unmarried partner treatment was not captured, so the conservative baseline is marriage before dependent filing. [src-253]
- **Temporary protection / Ukraine-specific humanitarian status**: no Malaysia Ukraine-specific temporary-protection route was captured. Malaysia planning should be ordinary entry plus DE Rantau / other pass routes, not an EU-style post-2027 protection bridge.

#### After 4 March 2027

- Malaysia is outside the EU temporary-protection framework. The relevant question is whether the couple can hold an ordinary Malaysian status after any EU/Polish temporary-protection planning horizon.

The next legal blocker is whether a remote IT worker can later move to Employment Pass, Residence Pass-Talent, Entry Permit, Malaysia My Second Home, company/self-employment, or another durable status without a local employer. #### PR and citizenship

- Malaysian Immigration describes an Entry Permit as a facility for foreign nationals to enter and reside legally in Malaysia without any time limit, and calls it the highest privilege granted by the Malaysian government to a foreign national. Listed permanent-residence application categories include spouse of a Malaysian citizen, child of a Malaysian citizen, and fully foreign national. [src-254]
- The public Entry Permit page does not by itself show that a DE Rantau holder can become a fully foreign national PR applicant or that Professional Visit Pass time counts. This is a major long-term verification gap. [src-254]
- The National Registration Department's Article 19 naturalization page requires applicants aged 21+ to have resided in Malaysia for at least 10 years in a 12-year period, including the immediate 12 months before application, intend to reside permanently, be of good character, have adequate Malay-language knowledge, have two citizen referees, and file Form C. [src-255]

### 5.2. Climate {status: deep, depth: 2, last_updated: 2026-06-05, dod: passed}

> **DoD**: January/July temperatures in 3+ cities; sunny days; humidity; year-round comfort.

- **Country baseline**: Malaysia is equatorial: hot, humid, and rainy throughout the year. Climate to Travel says highs are usually about 30/31 C around November-January and 32/33 C around March-August, with nights around 23/25 C and almost never below 20 C. Rain is frequent and there is no true dry season. [src-256]
- **Kuala Lumpur**: Climate to Travel describes Kuala Lumpur as tropical, hot and humid all year, with no dry season. Its temperature table gives January about 23.8/32.6 C min/max and July about 24.4/32.8 C; rainfall is still material even in the less rainy June-August period, and thunderstorms can cause flooding. [src-256]
- **Penang / George Town**: the west-coast Penang baseline is still hot year-round; January-February are the best months, with roughly 70 mm/month rainfall, while April-November is much wetter. This is useful for winter scouting but not a dry Mediterranean-style climate. [src-256]
- **Johor Bahru / southern peninsula**: Climate to Travel groups Johor Bahru with the interior/southern peninsula pattern: hot and humid, with February, June and July among the less rainy months but no clearly preferable dry season. [src-256]
- **Sun / clear days**: Kuala Lumpur has about 2,200 sunshine hours/year, and Malaysia's wettest areas can be around 1,800 hours/year while the least rainy areas can reach about 2,500 hours/year. WeatherSpark's Malaysia comparison gives monthly clearer-sky percentages for Kuala Lumpur, George Town, and Kuching (used here as an East Malaysia / wetter-coast comparison point). Converting those monthly percentages by month length gives only about **52 clearer-sky day-equivalent days/year for Kuala Lumpur**, **57 for George Town**, and **57 for Kuching**. This is a medium-confidence clearer-sky proxy, not an official sunny-day count. [src-256][src-279]
- **Practical comfort verdict**: Malaysia avoids cold winters entirely and has warm seas, but the couple should expect year-round humidity, thunderstorms, wet-season disruption, and much lower sunshine reliability than Mediterranean / dry subtropical countries. It may be comfortable for people who tolerate humid tropics, less so if the goal is dry warmth or predictable clear skies.

### 5.3. Taxes {status: partial, depth: 1, last_updated: 2026-06-11, dod: partial}

> **DoD**: effective rate at $3000/mo computed; registration procedure for applicable regime; gross->net example; new-resident reliefs; marriage tax effect.

#### Tax-residence and source baseline

- PwC's Malaysia individual summary states that both resident and non-resident individuals are taxed on income accruing in or derived from Malaysia, and that resident individuals are also subject to foreign-sourced income received in Malaysia. PwC's residence page uses the usual 182-day calendar-year threshold for individual tax residence. [src-465][src-466]
- For a DE Rantau holder physically working from Malaysia, the conservative screening assumption is **do not assume zero Malaysian tax**. PwC says employment income is taxable when work is performed in Malaysia regardless of where payment is made; for foreign-client freelance/business income, the exact source classification and whether any foreign-sourced-income exemption applies remain accountant / Inland Revenue Board application-prep checks. [src-467]
- Non-residents are taxed at a flat 30% on taxable income; residents use graduated rates and can claim personal reliefs. This makes the 182-day residency posture important for any stay beyond a short bridge. [src-465][src-466]

#### USD 3,000/month worked example

- FX snapshot: ExchangeRate-API reported USD 1 = MYR 4.06831 on 2026-06-11, so USD 3,000/month is about **MYR 12,205/month** or **MYR 146,460/year**. [src-470]
- Resident-rate stress test: applying PwC's 2026 resident PIT bands and only the basic MYR 9,000 self relief gives chargeable income of about MYR 137,460/year and PIT of about **MYR 18,765/year** (**MYR 1,564/month**). Screening net is therefore about **MYR 10,641/month** or **USD 2,616/month** before accountant, SST, social-security, health-insurance, and immigration-file costs. [src-465][src-468][src-470]
- Non-resident taxable-income downside: if the same MYR 12,205/month is taxable while non-resident, the 30% flat rate would leave about **MYR 8,544/month** (**USD 2,100/month**) before other costs. Treat this as a downside sensitivity, not the default if the couple becomes Malaysian tax resident. [src-465][src-470]
- Employee-style social-security sensitivity: PwC says non-Malaysian employees have a 2% employee EPF contribution and 2% employer contribution, while SOCSO applicability for foreign workers depends on the employment or assignment arrangement and must be considered case-by-case. If a 2% employee EPF-like deduction applied to the current income, resident net would fall to about **MYR 10,397/month** (**USD 2,556/month**). This is not a proven freelancer/DE Rantau result. [src-469][src-470]

#### Registration, filing, reliefs, and marriage

- Malaysia uses calendar-year taxation. PwC says individuals self-assess; returns are due by 30 April without business income and 30 June with business income, with e-filing available. A DE Rantau freelancer should budget for Malaysian tax-adviser onboarding rather than relying on payroll withholding. [src-469]
- Resident personal reliefs for YA 2026 include MYR 9,000 self relief and MYR 4,000 spouse relief under joint assessment; spouses are generally assessed separately, but a resident wife / Malaysian-citizen wife can elect joint assessment with the husband, and a husband with no income can elect joint assessment under the wife's name. The couple's likely one-earner posture may get some relief after marriage, but exact foreign-spouse eligibility and joint-assessment mechanics need adviser confirmation. [src-468][src-469]
- No Malaysia-specific digital-nomad tax holiday was captured. DE Rantau solves immigration only for up to 24 months; it does not by itself prove a tax exemption or a durable tax-registration route. [src-253][src-465]
- SST / indirect tax: PwC's other-taxes page gives sales tax rates of 5% or 10% by goods class and service tax generally 8% (6% for listed services), with expanded scope from 1 July 2025. Whether a foreign-client IT freelancer on DE Rantau has any SST registration, e-invoice, or export-service reporting exposure remains application-prep/accountant work. [src-469]

#### Tax verdict for screening

Malaysia is not a tax-free default for this couple. A conservative resident PIT stress test leaves roughly **USD 2,616/month** before non-tax costs, which is still plausible for a bridge country if rent and healthcare are moderate. The tax section stays partial because the key filing question is not the rate table; it is whether the couple's exact DE Rantau / foreign-client IT income is Malaysian-source employment/business income, exempt foreign-sourced income, or another category, and what social-security / SST / e-invoice obligations attach. Added `vq-115` for that application-prep tax-structure gap.
