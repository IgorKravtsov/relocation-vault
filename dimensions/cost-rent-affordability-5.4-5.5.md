## Scope

This map groups the already captured cost-of-living and rent baselines for the couple's current profile: about USD 3,000/month gross income, one remote IT earner, one student partner, and a need for a modest two-room / one-bedroom-plus-living-room apartment. It is a synthesis input, not a final country ranking, visit order, lease recommendation, or live-listing substitute.

## Pressure buckets

- **Comfortable if route works**: captured rent and basic living-cost evidence leaves a comparatively wide margin at USD 3,000/month, subject to normal city and lifestyle discipline.
- **Manageable with city discipline**: the country can screen as workable if the couple avoids the most expensive city/seasonal market and keeps rent controlled.
- **Tight / city-sensitive**: rent plus ordinary costs can consume the budget quickly; final-city choice, tax net, healthcare/insurance, and deposits are decisive.
- **High rent or one-income pressure**: the captured baseline shows major pressure in the practical cities or a narrow after-tax budget; not a rejection by itself, but a strong stress flag.
- **Tax/route-gated despite affordable rent**: headline rents may be reasonable, but legal-route income gates, weak settlement route, or tax/status problems are the binding constraint.
