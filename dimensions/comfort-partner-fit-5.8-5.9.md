## Scope

This map groups the already captured comfort-of-life and partner/student baselines for the couple's current profile: one Ukrainian remote IT earner, one Ukrainian student partner, current income around USD 3,000/month, unmarried but willing to marry if it materially improves legalisation, and possible future child planning. It is a synthesis input, not a final country ranking, relationship advice, university advice, safety advice, or visit order.

## Fit buckets

- **Marriage/dependent path usable; adaptation manageable**: the safer partner baseline is marriage or registered family dependency, but day-to-day services, student continuity, or diaspora/support factors make the comfort file screenable.
- **Marriage likely needed; language/bureaucracy is the main friction**: the legal partner file is workable only with marriage/family status or independent eligibility, while local-language bureaucracy and school/health/service adaptation dominate daily life.
- **Independent-status fallback important**: unmarried-partner recognition is weak, route-specific, or not captured, so the partner should not rely only on dependency; independent TP, student, work, DN, or ordinary residence options need to be preserved.
- **Bridge/status-capped partner risk**: the main route is time-capped, income-gated, or weak as a settlement ladder; partner inclusion may work for a bridge but does not solve durable residence.
- **High adaptation / distance / social-norm pressure**: comfort may be workable in selected cities, but language, distance from Ukraine, safety filters, climate, cultural norms, or private-service dependence materially raise one-income household friction.

## Synthesis-use guardrails

- Use this file to spot partner-status and day-to-day adaptation patterns, not to rank countries or override assigned screening tiers.
- Do not treat a warm/comfortable city or English-language convenience as proof of durable partner residence; route durability remains in `dimensions/route-durability-5.1.md`.
- Do not assume unmarried cohabitation is accepted unless the country profile already captured route-specific evidence. For screening, marriage/family dependency or independent status is the conservative baseline in most countries.
- Treat student transfer, local-university enrollment, exact work rights for the student partner, partner-dependent income add-ons, and final-city safety/community checks as application-prep or later deep-dive work unless a later profile iteration upgrades them.
- Preserve the distinction between bridge comfort and settlement fit: a route can be comfortable for 6-24 months while still weak for post-2027 residence, PR, or citizenship planning.
