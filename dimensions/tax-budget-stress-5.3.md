## Scope

This map groups the already captured tax-section screening baselines for the couple's current income profile: one remote IT earner at about USD 3,000/month, one non-working student partner, and possible marriage if it materially helps. It is a synthesis input, not a final country ranking, tax recommendation, or filing plan.

## Stress buckets

- **Screening-positive / low-tax stress**: the first-pass model leaves most of USD 3,000/month intact or the tax burden is structurally light, but immigration and adviser checks may still be decisive.
- **Manageable but adviser-dependent**: a plausible tax baseline remains workable on USD 3,000/month, but exact regime classification, social contributions, VAT/place-of-supply, or immigration-status fit must be confirmed.
- **Tight / one-income stress**: taxes and contributions leave a usable but narrow one-income budget; city choice, rent discipline, and accountant confirmation are material.
- **High tax/status stress**: the conservative model or route-status uncertainty can push the budget close to or below a safe floor for two adults, even before rent, insurance, and application costs.
- **Tax-favorable but immigration-gated**: tax burden itself is light, but the legal route has an income or settlement blocker that prevents the tax result from being decisive.
