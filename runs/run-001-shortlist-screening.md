### Key Findings

#### 1. Temporary Protection Status (Critical for All EU/Schengen Candidates)
- **EU Temporary Protection for Ukrainians extended until 4 March 2027** (Council Decision, March 2025).
- The Temporary Protection Directive (2001/55/EC) grants the right to stay, work, and study in any EU member state. Beneficiaries can choose their destination freely.
- **However**, a beneficiary can generally be registered for temporary protection in **only one** EU member state at a time.
- **Open Question (CRITICAL)**: If one partner already holds a Polish *karta pobytu* (regular residence permit, NOT temporary protection), does this block applying for temporary protection in another EU country? Does it matter whether the Polish permit is a standard residence permit vs. temporary protection? This requires lawyer verification. [Flagged in open-questions.md]
- Post-March 2027: The Temporary Protection Directive allows a maximum of 3 years total. What happens after March 2027 is currently country-dependent. Some countries (e.g., Germany, Czechia) have announced transition pathways; others have not. [Flagged in open-questions.md]

#### 3. Climate Filter
- **Warm year-round / Mediterranean**: Portugal (south), Spain (south/east), Malta, Cyprus, Greece, Italy (south), Croatia (coast)
- **Warm summers, cold winters** (continental — FAILS climate preference): Czechia, Slovakia, Poland, Hungary, Romania (inland), Bulgaria (inland), Serbia (inland)
- **Coastal exceptions**: Croatia coast, Montenegro coast, Black Sea coast of Bulgaria/Romania are significantly milder than inland

#### 4. Legalization Without Local Employer (Preliminary)
- **Digital nomad visas**: Available in Portugal, Spain, Croatia, Greece, Cyprus, Malta, Hungary, Romania, Czechia(ish — živno), Italy
- **Self-employed / freelancer residence**: Most EU countries allow this, but minimum income thresholds vary
- **Key constraint**: $3000/mo (€2750) may be below minimum income requirements for some digital nomad visas (e.g., Portugal ~€3280, Spain ~€2000+, Greece ~€3500, Malta ~€3500, Croatia ~€2600)
- **For non-EU**: Georgia (1-year visa-free + easy residence), Montenegro (residence via property or company), Serbia (residence via company), Turkey (digital nomad visa ~$3000/mo)

#### 5. Taxes (Very Preliminary — To Be Deep-Dived)
- Known favorable regimes for remote workers: Greece (50% tax break for 7 years), Portugal (NHR being modified but still exists for some categories), Italy (flat tax for new residents), Cyprus (non-dom regime), Bulgaria (10% flat), Romania (10% flat for micro), Hungary (flat 15%), Georgia (1% turnover tax for small business)

## Shortlist Decision

### INCLUDED in Shortlist (9 countries):
1. **Portugal** — EU/Schengen, warm south, digital nomad visa, NHR tax benefits, moderate cost (tight in Lisbon), strong Ukrainian community. Risk: NHR changes, rising rents.
2. **Spain** — EU/Schengen, warm coasts, digital nomad visa since 2023, lower cost than PT in some regions, strong Ukrainian diaspora. Risk: bureaucracy, Beckham Law less favorable now.
3. **Greece** — EU/Schengen, warm, very affordable outside Athens, digital nomad visa, 50% tax break for remote workers. Risk: economic volatility, islands expensive.
4. **Croatia** — EU/Schengen (since 2023), warm coast, pioneer digital nomad visa, flat tax self-employed option. Risk: seasonal cost spikes, language barrier.
5. **Cyprus** — EU/Schengen(ish — not Schengen yet but close), warm year-round, non-dom tax, English widely spoken. Risk: expensive in Limassol, no Schengen access for travel.
6. **Italy** — EU/Schengen, warm south, flat tax for new residents, strong culture. Risk: north is expensive, bureaucracy, language.
7. **Bulgaria** — EU member, extremely cheap, 10% flat tax, Black Sea coast is mild. Risk: cold inland winters, language barrier, lower wages if seeking local work.
8. **Romania** — EU member, very cheap, flat tax, growing tech scene. Risk: cold winters inland, language barrier.
9. **Malta** — EU/Schengen, warm, English official, digital nomad visa. Risk: very small, expensive housing, tight budget.

### EXCLUDED (with reasoning):
- **Czechia** — Cold winters, rents rising in Prague, language barrier. Still viable but lower priority vs. warm options.
- **Slovenia** — Expensive for budget vs. value, Alpine/cold winters, small market. Lower priority.
- **Hungary** — Cold winters, political stability concerns, weakening rule of law. Lower priority.
- **Slovakia** — Cold winters, limited options, lower priority.
- **Poland** — Already has karta pobytu there; while important to deep-dive as a baseline, not a "new destination." Cold winters. Will be covered in a separate topic.
- **Montenegro** — Not EU. Beautiful and cheap, but limited long-term legal path. Kept as "second-tier" candidate.
- **Serbia** — Not EU. Cheap and warm, but limited long-term path. Kept as "second-tier."
- **Turkey** — Not EU. Inflation volatility, complex politics. Kept as "second-tier."
- **Georgia** — Not EU. Very cheap, easy entry, but limited EU path. Kept as "second-tier."
