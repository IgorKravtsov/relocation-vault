### Key Findings

#### Finding 1: Clear Transition Pathway from Temporary Protection [High Confidence]
Greek Law 5078/2023 Article 194 explicitly allows TP holders to apply for ANY residence permit under the Immigration Code without needing an entry visa. The new permit replaces TP. This is one of the most concrete post-2027 transition mechanisms found in any EU country.

#### Finding 2: Digital Nomad Visa May Have Income Threshold Above Budget [Medium Confidence]
The Greek DN visa under Law 4825/2021 Article 80 is widely reported to require ~€3,500/mo net income. The applicant's $3,000/mo (~€2,750) may fall short. However, alternatives exist: TP → self-employed permit, or direct application for self-employed residence.

#### Finding 3: 50% Tax Reduction Can Dramatically Lower Tax Burden [Medium Confidence]
Law 4712/2020 (as amended by 4921/2022) offers a 50% reduction on employment/self-employment income for up to 7 years for new tax residents from treaty countries (Ukraine qualifies). Estimated effective rate on €36K/year could be as low as ~8% vs ~22% without reduction. Exact calculation needs Greek tax accountant.

#### Finding 4: Very Comfortable Budget in Greece [Medium Confidence]
With €2,750/mo income, estimated total expenses for a couple (2BR rent + living + insurance) are €1,900-2,100/mo, leaving €650-850 surplus. This is based on country-wide Numbeo data, not yet verified city-by-city.

#### Finding 5: Partner Can Join as Dependent [Medium Confidence]
Married partners have straightforward dependent rights. Unmarried partners face uncertainty — Greek law may not recognize cohabitation for non-EU family reunification.
